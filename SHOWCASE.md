# NANCY HUB — SHOWCASE

## THE PROBLEM

Nancy's team ran on WhatsApp, Slack, and a shared calendar. No single source of truth. Priorities arrived from every direction — bajillions of them, scattered across channels — and without a way to organize them, the team stayed reactive. Work got done in the wrong order: someone executing tomorrow's task while today's deadline burned. Finding information meant digging through message threads. Finding who owned what meant asking someone. The overhead wasn't laziness — it was the cost of having no system.

---

## WHAT WAS BUILT

A private internal hub — 9 pages, built in vanilla HTML/CSS/JS with Supabase — designed around how a small content-driven team actually works.

**Task Hub** — Kanban board with priority flagging, assignees, and due dates. The team's main workspace. Functional and in active daily use.

**Learning Hub** — Internal knowledge base. Guides, references, and team knowledge in one searchable place instead of someone's head or a buried Slack message.

**Events** — Milestone-driven event tracker. Back-schedules key dates (freight cutoffs, sign-off deadlines, go-live) from the event date automatically. 2026 calendar pre-loaded.

**Projects** — Campaign and Universal Trend tracker. Two types with separate milestone templates: campaigns get brief/budget/concept/launch phases; trend moments (Coachella, Mother's Day, Black Friday) get content-focused phases from 28 days out to go-live.

**Social** — Content studio for scheduling and planning. Still being built out.

**Partnerships** — Creator/collaboration tracking. Still needs work.

**Dashboard, Brand Guidelines, Onboarding** — Supporting infrastructure.

---

## IMPACT

*Projected, not yet measured. This is an honest label.*

- Priorities visible in one place instead of three channels → less reactive, faster triage
- Task ownership explicit and persistent → less time spent asking "who's handling this?"
- Milestone back-scheduling → deadlines surface before they're urgent, not after
- Knowledge centralized → less time spent re-explaining or re-finding information

*Current state: hub is in internal testing with two people before full team rollout. Task Hub and Learning Hub are the most validated. Everything else is still being proven.*

---

## DAILY PROGRESS

The hub is live and being actively tested, but still being shaped. It was built fast, by one person, while the team was already using it. The scope is ambitious — deliberately so. Some pages are proven. Some are experiments. The underlying architecture is solid (Supabase, shared component library, consistent patterns across pages), which means adding and refining features doesn't require starting over.

Yesterday: Projects tab completely rebuilt with type-aware forms, auto-generated milestones, and a full 2026 trend calendar.

---

## HONEST ASSESSMENT

The hub works. Task Hub and Learning Hub are genuinely useful today. The rest exists but hasn't been proven in daily practice yet — some of it is feature-complete, some is still finding its shape.

The biggest risk isn't the tech. It's clarity. There are a lot of functions. Not all of them are obvious to a new user. The "not clean enough" feeling is real, and it's worth naming: this is a working prototype being refined in production, not a polished product handed over to a client. The ambition is right. The execution is catching up.

What this is not: a tool that replaced chaos overnight.
What this is: a foundation that makes replacing chaos possible.
